Virat Kohli

Indian Cricketer | Former Captain | Global Sports Icon

Introduction Early Life Career Captaincy Records Personal Life Legacy

Introduction

Virat Kohli is one of the greatest cricketers in the history of the sport and a global icon of modern cricket. Known for his aggressive mindset, consistency, and unmatched passion, he has redefined batting standards across all formats of the game.

Early Life and Family

Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi, India, into a Punjabi Hindu family. His father, Prem Nath Kohli, was a criminal lawyer, and his mother, Saroj Kohli, is a homemaker.

He joined the West Delhi Cricket Academy in 1998 and trained under coach Rajkumar Sharma. After his father’s death in 2006, Kohli displayed extraordinary mental strength by playing a Ranji Trophy match the next day.

Cricket Career

Kohli captained India to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 World Cup and made his ODI debut later that year. Over time, he became one of the most dependable batsmen in world cricket, earning the title “Chase Master.”

Captaincy Era

  • Led India to No. 1 Test ranking
  • Most successful Indian Test captain
  • First Indian captain to win a Test series in Australia
  • Introduced a strong fitness culture

Records and Achievements

  • One of the highest century scorers in international cricket
  • Fastest to multiple ODI milestones
  • ICC Cricketer of the Year (multiple times)
  • Arjuna Award, Padma Shri, Khel Ratna

Personal Life & Philanthropy

Virat Kohli married actress Anushka Sharma in December 2017. He is also a successful entrepreneur and runs the Virat Kohli Foundation, supporting underprivileged children and sports development.

Legacy

Virat Kohli symbolizes passion, discipline, and modern professionalism. His journey continues to inspire millions of aspiring athletes around the world.
